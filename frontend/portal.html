Philippine Disaster Data Foundation

ReliefSync's ML model is trained on DSWD/NDRRMC-grounded historical patterns, rather than generic global data. The baseline multipliers are sourced from:

  • NDRRMC (National Disaster Risk Reduction and Management Council)
    Incident report structure, camp population ranges, and supply demand baselines used in actual typhoon operations in Luzon. ndrrmc.gov.ph ↗
  • PAGASA Tropical Cyclone Wind Signal Classification (Signal 1–5)
    The Philippine Atmospheric, Geophysical and Astronomical Services Administration's official storm signal system is our weather severity input. Signal 5 = catastrophic (185+ km/h), Signal 1 = minimal (60 km/h). pagasa.dost.gov.ph ↗
  • SPHERE Humanitarian Standards (2018)
    The internationally recognized minimum standards for disaster relief operations. Water: 15L per person/day. Rice: 400g per person/day. These form our base consumption rates. spherestandards.org ↗
  • DSWD (Dept. of Social Welfare and Development) Family Food Packs
    Standard Philippine government relief pack composition used to calibrate our rice and supply units. dswd.gov.ph ↗

Relief Officer Quick Guide

How to use ReliefSync to make dispatch decisions during a typhoon.

1

Select Your Evacuation Center

Choose the camp you are managing from the dropdown on the left. Each camp has a different number of evacuees, stock levels, and PAGASA signal exposure. Try all 3 to see CRITICAL, WARNING, and STABLE examples.

2

Click "Generate 72-Hour Forecast"

ReliefSync's ML model analyzes the population, PAGASA storm signal, and supply type to predict how many units will be needed over the next 72 hours. This call goes to a local Python/Flask server running the AI model.

3

Read the Status Cards (Top Row)

CRITICAL = Less than 30% of needed supplies are on-site. Dispatch a truck immediately.
WARNING = 30–60% covered. Dispatch today before roads become impassable.
STABLE = Over 60% covered. Monitor and prepare for next cycle.

4

Read the Depletion Chart

The chart shows how fast each supply runs out if no new truck arrives. The steep drop from 12h–36h represents typhoon peak — when roads are blocked and consumption is highest. When a line crosses the red dashed line, that supply is in critical shortage.

5

Use the Packing Manifest to Order Supplies

The table shows exactly how many units to put on the dispatch truck. The Coverage Bar is color-coded: RED means almost empty, GREEN means sufficient. The Recommended Dispatch column is the number to radio to the DSWD warehouse.

6

Launch the Warehouse Loading View

Click "Load Truck ↗" to open the warehouse Kanban board. Drag supply cards from "To Load" → "In Progress" → "Loaded". Once all items are loaded, the dispatch button unlocks and you can confirm the truck has left.

7

View ML Insights for Transparency

Click "ML Insights" to see a step-by-step breakdown of how the AI computed each number: Base consumption + PAGASA storm surge bonus = Total demand. This builds trust with LGU officials and NDRRMC coordinators.
